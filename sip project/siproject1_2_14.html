remedy for motion sickness Motion sickness, also known as kinetosis, is a condition that occurs when the body's inner ear, eyes, and sensory receptors in the muscles and skin send conflicting signals to the brain about motion and position. This can cause symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and sweating.
Here are some remedies that may help alleviate symptoms of motion sickness:
Medications: Over-the-counter medications such as dimenhydrinate (Dramamine) and meclizine (Bonine) can help relieve symptoms of motion sickness.
Ginger: Ginger has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties and can help reduce nausea and vomiting associated with motion sickness. You can take ginger in supplement form, or drink ginger tea or chew on ginger candies.
Acupressure: Applying pressure to the P6 point, which is located on the inside of the wrist, can help reduce symptoms of motion sickness.
Essential oils: Peppermint, lavender, and ginger essential oils can help reduce nausea and vomiting associated with motion sickness.
Deep breathing and visualization: Focusing on deep breathing and visualizing a peaceful scene can help reduce symptoms of motion sickness.
Avoiding certain foods: Avoiding spicy, greasy, or fatty foods before travel can help reduce the likelihood of motion sickness.
It's important to note that the best remedy may vary from person to person, so it's a good idea to try a few different options to see what works best for you.
Ginger-Lemon Tea: Ingredients: 1 inch of fresh ginger root, grated or sliced
1/2 lemon, juiced
1 cup of water
1 tsp honey (optional)
Cardamom-Cumin Tea: Ingredients: 1 tsp cumin seeds
1 tsp cardamom pods
1 cup of water
1 tsp honey (optional)

ghee-ginger-rock: ingredients : 1 tsp ghee (clarified butter)
1 tsp ginger juice (freshly squeezed from grated ginger)
A pinch of rock salt (or to taste)
Recipe for Ginger - Lemon tea:
Bring water to a boil in a small saucepan.
Add the ginger and lemon juice to the water and reduce the heat to low.
Allow the mixture to simmer for about 5-10 minutes.
Remove from heat and strain the tea through a fine mesh strainer.
Add honey (if using)
Drink the tea, before or during the journey for best results.
This tea can be consumed before or during the journey to alleviate the symptoms of motion sickness. The ginger in the tea can help to settle the stomach, while the lemon provides a refreshing and invigorating aroma which can alleviate nausea.
Recipie for Cardamom-Cumin Tea Bring water to a boil in a small saucepan.
Add the cumin seeds and cardamom pods to the water and reduce the heat to low.
Allow the mixture to simmer for about 5-10 minutes.
Remove from heat and strain the tea through a fine mesh strainer.
Add honey (if using) Drink the tea, before or during the journey for best results.
Cardamom and cumin are both known for their carminative properties which can help to settle the stomach and alleviate nausea and vomiting. Additionally, cardamom has a refreshing and invigorating aroma that can help to alleviate feelings of nausea.
Ghee-ginger-rock salt:
Mix 1 tsp of ghee, 1 tsp of ginger juice and a pinch of rock salt.
Consume this mixture before the journey to prevent motion sickness.
Ghee is known for its calming properties, ginger helps to settle the stomach and rock salt helps to balance the electrolytes in the body.
