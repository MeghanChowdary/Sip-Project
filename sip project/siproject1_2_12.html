Remedy In Ayurvedic medicine, stress is considered an imbalance in the body's doshas, or energies. Ayurvedic remedies for stress aim to restore balance to the doshas and promote overall health and well-being. Here are a few popular Ayurvedic remedies for stress:
Herbs: Certain herbs, such as ashwagandha, brahmi, and Shankhapushpi, have been traditionally used in Ayurveda to promote relaxation and reduce stress. These herbs can be taken in supplement form or as part of a herbal tea blend.
Aromatherapy : Essential oils, such as lavender and jasmine, have been used in Ayurveda to promote relaxation and reduce stress. Inhaling these oils or using them in massage can help calm the mind and reduce stress.
Yoga and Meditation: These practices are considered essential in Ayurveda for promoting physical and mental well-being. Regular practice can help reduce stress and promote relaxation.
Diet: A balanced and nourishing diet that is tailored to your individual constitution, or dosha, can help reduce stress and promote overall health.
Lifestyle Changes: Making changes to your daily habits and routines can also help reduce stress. Ayurveda recommends regular exercise, adequate sleep, and reducing exposure to stressful situations.

recipes for some of the Ayurvedic remedies for stress: ashwagandha tea recipie:
Boil the water.

Let the tea steep for 5-10 minutes.
Strain the tea and drink it while it's still warm.
Method:

Diet:
Spiced Rice:
Ingredients:
1 cup of brown rice
1 teaspoon of ghee (clarified butter)
1 teaspoon of ginger powder
1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
Salt to taste
lavander oil recipie:
Rinse the rice and place it in a saucepan with 2 cups of water.
Add the ginger powder, turmeric powder, and salt to the saucepan.
Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and let the rice simmer for about 20 minutes or until fully cooked.
Stir in the ghee and serve hot
The appropriate dosage for each Ayurvedic remedy for stress can vary depending on a number of factors, including your age, weight, and overall health.
It's important to consult with an Ayurvedic practitioner or your doctor to determine the right dosage for you.
That being said, here is some general information about dosages for some common Ayurvedic remedies for stress:
Herbs:
Ashwagandha:
The recommended dose of ashwagandha is typically between 500mg and 1000mg per day, taken in capsule form.
Brahmi:
The recommended dose of brahmi is typically between 300mg and 500mg per day, taken in capsule form.
Shankhapushpi:
The recommended dose of shankhapushpi is typically between 500mg and 1000mg per day, taken in capsule form.
Essential oils:
Lavender:

Ginger and turmeric can be added to your cooking or taken in supplement form. The recommended daily dose of ginger is 4g to 6g and the recommended daily dose of turmeric is 500mg to 2000mg.
Adaptogenic herbs:
The recommended daily dose of ashwagandha and ginseng varies depending on the product and the individual, but it is typically between 300mg and 500mg per day.
