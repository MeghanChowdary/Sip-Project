CUMIN
Cumin is a spice that comes from the Cuminum cyminum plant. It is native to Asia, Africa, and Europe. However, people all around the world use it to flavor meals.
properties of cumin
PROPERTIES
1)Cholesterol In one study, cumin powder dissolved in yogurt helped reduce “bad” (LDL) cholesterol and triglycerides while increasing “good” (HDL) cholesterol. High LDL cholesterol increases your chances of health problems like heart attack or strokes. High triglyceride levels can make you more likely to have heart problems. HDL helps to supply LDL to liver to flush it out from the body. Several studies have shown that cumin help people control their cholesterol levels. Cumin effectively lowered oxidized LDL cholesterol in patients with already high cholesterol.
2)Diabetes Type 2 diabetes occurs when a person’s body no longer responds to insulin correctly. This is called insulin resistance. Insulin is a hormone that regulates the movement of blood glucose, or sugar, into cells, which use it as energy. If sugar cannot enter the cells,too much glucose collects in the blood,and body cannot use it as energy. Cyminum supplement in patients with diabetes type II may decrease the serum levels of insulin, FBS, and glycosylated hemoglobin.
3)Regular Bowel Movements Irritable bowel syndrome is one of the most common gastrointestinal disorders occurs due to altered bowel habits or changes in stool consistency. Cumin extract can be used for treating the cramps, digestive spasms, nausea, and bloating associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Consuming cumin extract can reduce constipation and diarrhea.
4)Reduce Stress Cumin helps in fighting the effects of stress by acting as an antioxidant. Cumin can help your body by stimulating your central nervous system to be more effective. This can result in a sharper memory and greater control over your limbs.
5)Memory Packed with a bunch of minerals and vitamins such as riboflavin, vitamin B6, zeaxanthin, and niacin in cumin,consuming it is beneficial for the brain to function properly. Cumin helps in promoting better mental health and sharpen the memory by nourishing the brain cells.
6)Treats Haemorrhoids Cumin is a rich source of dietary fiber, exhibits carminative, anti-fungal, and anti-microbial properties. The essential oils cumin aldehyde and pyrazines in jeera act as a natural laxative, aids in curing infections or wounds in the excretory system and relieves hemorrhoids.

medicinal history of cumin
HISTORY Cumin originated in Western Asia where it was cultivated since biblical times. Today, India and Iran are the main producers of cumin worldwide.
It is also cultivated in Argentina, Morocco, the Ukraine, Egypt, Lebanon, Malta, Mexico, Afghanistan, Pakistan, Turkey, Central America, and Central Asia.
The oldest reference to cumin dates back 5,000 years as a mummification ingredient for the bodies of Egyptian pharaohs.
The ancient Greeks kept cumin at the dining table in its own container. Superstition during the Middle Ages cited that cumin kept chickens and lovers from wandering.
It was also believed that a happy life awaited the bride and groom who carried cumin seed throughout the wedding ceremony.
later on cumin was used as medicine herb. The seeds of the plant are used to make medicine.
People take cumin for digestion problems including diarrhea, colic, bowel spasms, and gas.
Cumin is also used to increase urine flow to relieve bloating and to start menstruation.
