There are several remedies that can help alleviate symptoms of allergies, including:
Medications: Over-the-counter antihistamines, such as loratadine (Claritin) and cetirizine (Zyrtec), can help relieve symptoms such as runny nose, sneezing, and itchy eyes. Nasal sprays, such as fluticasone (Flonase) and cromolyn (NasalCrom), can also help relieve nasal congestion.
Allergen immunotherapy: Also known as allergy shots, this treatment involves administering small amounts of the allergen to the individual over time to help build up their tolerance to it.
Avoiding allergens: Identifying and avoiding the substances that trigger your allergies can help reduce your symptoms.
Saline nasal irrigation: Rinsing the nasal passages with a saline solution can help remove allergens and mucus from the nose.
Nasal corticosteroid sprays: These sprays reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and can be effective in reducing symptoms of allergic rhinitis.
Natural remedies: Some natural remedies such as local honey, quercetin, and butterbur have been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce symptoms of allergies.

Turmeric-Neem face mask:
Ingredients:
1 tsp turmeric powder
1 tsp neem powder
2 tsp aloe vera gel
2 tsp honey
1 tsp yogurt

herbal oil
Ingredients:

1/4 cup sesame oil
1 tsp licorice powder
1 tsp turmeric powder
1 tsp neem powder
2-3 drops of lavender essential oil (optional)
kashayam Ingredients:
1 tsp licorice root powder
1 tsp turmeric powder
1 tsp neem leaves powder
1 cup of water

Turmeric-Neem face mask:In a small mixing bowl, combine the turmeric powder, neem powder, aloe vera gel, honey, and yogurt.
Mix the ingredients together until they form a smooth paste.
Apply the paste to your face and let it sit for 15-20 minutes.
Rinse the mask off with lukewarm water and pat your face dry.
This face mask can be used once or twice a week to reduce redness, itching, and other symptoms of skin allergies.

It's important to note that turmeric can temporarily stain the skin yellow and before applying the mask to face, it is better to do a patch test on small area to check for any adverse reactions. Also, people with sensitive skin should avoid using neem as it may cause irritation. herbal oil Heat the sesame oil in a small saucepan over low heat. Add the licorice powder, turmeric powder, and neem powder to the oil and stir well.
Allow the mixture to simmer for about 10 minutes.
Remove from heat and add 2-3 drops of lavender essential oil (if using) Strain the oil through a fine mesh strainer and let it cool.
Apply the oil gently to the affected areas of the skin, massage it gently and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before washing it off.
This herbal oil can be used 2-3 times a week for best results. It is important to note that before using the oil, do a patch test to check for any adverse reactions.
kashayam
In a small saucepan, add 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil. Add the licorice root powder, turmeric powder, and neem leaves powder to the boiling water and reduce the heat to low.
Allow the mixture to simmer for about 10-15 minutes.
Strain the decoction through a fine mesh strainer and let it cool.
Drink the decoction, once or twice a day for best results.
It is important to note that before using the decoction, do a patch test to check for any adverse reactions. Also, please consult a qualified Ayurvedic practitioner before using any herbs.
Please keep in mind that these remedies are not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. If you have a skin allergy, it's important to consult with a doctor or allergist to get a proper diagnosis and treatment plan.
