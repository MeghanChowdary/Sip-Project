In Ayurvedic medicine, there are several remedies that can be used to reduce fever and relieve its symptoms:
Tulsi: Tulsi, also known as holy basil, is an herb that is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it effective in reducing fever. It can be consumed as a tea by boiling fresh or dried tulsi leaves in water.
Neem: Neem is another herb commonly used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat fever. It is believed to have antifungal, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, and can be taken as a tea or in capsule form.
Ginger: Ginger is believed to have antipyretic (fever-reducing) properties, as well as anti-inflammatory effects that can relieve pain and discomfort associated with fever. Ginger can be consumed as a tea or added to food as a spice.
Turmeric: Turmeric is another herb commonly used in Ayurvedic medicine that is believed to have antipyretic properties. It can be taken as a tea or in supplement form.
Ghee: Ghee is a type of clarified butter that is commonly used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat fever and other illnesses. It is believed to have nourishing and soothing effects on the body.
These remedies can be used in conjunction with other traditional medical treatments for fever.

Here are the ingredients for each of the Ayurvedic remedies for fever:
Tulsi tea: Fresh or dried tulsi leaves Water
Neem tea: Fresh or dried neem leaves Water
Ginger tea: Fresh ginger root Water
Turmeric tea: Fresh or dried turmeric root or turmeric powder Water
Ghee: Unsalted butter
Here are the recipes for each of the Ayurvedic remedies for fever:
Tulsi tea: Boil 2-3 fresh tulsi leaves or 1-2 teaspoons of dried tulsi leaves in a cup of water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool to a comfortable drinking temperature. Drink the tea while it's still warm.
Neem tea: Boil a handful of fresh or dried neem leaves in a cup of water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool to a comfortable drinking temperature. Drink the tea while it's still warm.
Ginger tea: Slice or grate 1-inch piece of fresh ginger root. Boil the ginger slices or grated ginger in a cup of water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool to a comfortable drinking temperature. Drink the tea while it's still warm.
Turmeric tea: Boil 1-2 teaspoons of fresh or dried turmeric root or turmeric powder in a cup of water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool to a comfortable drinking temperature. Drink the tea while it's still warm.
Ghee: Place a small saucepan on low heat and add 1 cup of unsalted butter. Let the butter melt and simmer until the solids at the bottom of the pan turn golden brown and the liquid on top is clear, about 15-20 minutes. Strain the ghee through a cheesecloth or fine mesh strainer into a clean, heat-resistant container. Store the ghee in an airtight container in a cool, dark place.
Here are the recommended dosages for each of the Ayurvedic remedies for fever:
Tulsi tea: Drink 1-2 cups of tulsi tea per day.
Neem tea: Drink 1-2 cups of neem tea per day.
Ginger tea: Drink 1-2 cups of ginger tea per day.
Turmeric tea: Drink 1-2 cups of turmeric tea per day.
Ghee: Take 1 teaspoon of ghee 1-2 times per day, either by itself or added to food.
