History


Amla, also known as Indian gooseberry, is a small, round fruit that is native to India and grows on the Indian gooseberry tree (Phyllanthus emblica). It is a rich source of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, and has a variety of medicinal and nutritional benefits.

properties of amla • Amla is high in vitamin C, making it a natural immune booster.
• It contains antioxidants that can help protect the body against oxidative stress and the effects of aging.
• Amla is also a good source of vitamin A, iron, and calcium.
• It has been traditionally used to support digestive health and may help with constipation and other digestive issues.
• Amla has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for centuries and is believed to have a number of medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial effects.

way to use amla • Amla can be consumed fresh, dried, or in the form of a juice or supplement.
• Fresh amla can be eaten raw or used in cooking, such as in chutneys or pickles.
• Dried amla can be ground into a powder and used as a spice or added to smoothies or other drinks.
• Amla juice can be purchased or made at home by blending fresh amla with water and straining the mixture.
• It can be consumed on its own or mixed with other juices or beverages.
• Amla supplements are available in the form of capsules, tablets, or extracts and can be taken according to the package instructions.
myths and facts about Amla
• Myth: Amla s sour and tastes bad.

• Fact: While amla does have a sour taste, it is also sweet and has a unique, citrus-like flavor.
Some people may find the taste unpleasant at first, but many people come to enjoy it over time.
medicinal history of Amla
• Amla has a long history of use in Ayurvedic medicine and is considered a "rasayana," or rejuvenating herb, due to its believed ability to promote healthy aging and overall well-being.
• It has been traditionally used to support the immune system, improve digestion, and promote healthy skin and hair.
• In modern times, amla has been studied for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. It has also been investigated for its potential role in managing diabetes, improving cardiovascular health, and supporting brain health.
